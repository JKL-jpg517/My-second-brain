# 4.1a Orthogonal Vectors and Subspaces

## Orthogonal Vectors

$v$ and $w$ are orthogonal iff:

$$v^{T}w = 0$$

## Orthogonal Subspaces

**Definition:** Two subspaces $V$ and $W$ are orthogonal if:

> $v^{T}w = 0$ for **every** $v \in V$ and **every** $w \in W$

Key word: *every*. Not "there exists some."

## The Two Orthogonal Pairs

| Subspace pair | Living in |
|---------------|-----------|
| Row space $\perp$ Nullspace | $\mathbb{R}^{n}$ |
| Column space $\perp$ $N(A^{T})$ | $\mathbb{R}^{m}$ |

## Proof: Row Space ⟂ Nullspace

The trick: **view $Ax$ as the dot product of each row of $A$ with $x$.**

- $x \in N(A) \implies Ax = 0$
- Each component of $Ax$ is a row of $A$ dotted with $x$
- So **every row** of $A$ is orthogonal to $x$
- The rows span the row space → any linear combination of rows is also orthogonal to $x$
- Therefore: row space $\perp$ nullspace ∎

The same argument with $A^{T}$ gives: column space $\perp$ $N(A^{T})$.
