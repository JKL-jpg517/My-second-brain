## Multiplication formulas&facts
- $AB$ is usually different from $BA$

- $A(B+C)=AB+AC$

## $A=CR$ : factorize A into *bones* and *formula*
$$
	A_{m\times n}=C_{m\times p}R_{p\times n}
$$
>[!tip]- Core and [[ A=CR]]
>-$C$(Columns):Contains the First $r$ Independent Columns of $A$
>-$R$(Rows): Tells how to produce $A$ from $C$



>[!example]-
>![p.29](Academic/Sources/数学/教辅/introduction-to-linear-algebra-6nbsped-1733146679-9781733146678_compress.pdf#page=39&rect=67,31,422,90&color=note)
>extreme case: rank=1
>-One indenpendent row[1 2 10 100] one independent column(1,3,2)$\implies$ all columns are multiples of (1,3,2) all rows are multiples of [1 2 10 100]

## How to find R and C?
premise: we view $A=CR$ in the perspective of $C$
- Method1 
    try every conlum of $A$ by dependency examination of previous columns(e.g solving fractions) 
> [!example]-
> - while finding the $C$ of $B$: ![p.32](Academic/Sources/数学/教辅/introduction-to-linear-algebra-6nbsped-1733146679-9781733146678_compress.pdf#page=42&rect=99,140,408,199&color=note)

- Method2: row-operate $A$ (a more systematic method)
    - ==row operations won't change the linear relationships of columns(feel it!)
    - $A$$\implies$ $U\implies$ $R_{{0}}$$\implies$ $R_{0}$ will reveal both $C$ and $R$
>[!Example]
>![p.33](Academic/Sources/数学/教辅/introduction-to-linear-algebra-6nbsped-1733146679-9781733146678_compress.pdf#page=43&rect=49,348,477,559&color=note)
>- the first operation on $U$: $(-\frac{1}{2})\times$ row2(it is still a linear row operation!!!); all rows of $R_{0}, U$ are combs of rows of $A$ $\implies$ ==The relative relationship between columns say the same throughout the process==
>- Some facts form the procdure:
>1. ==$U$: row3=zero$\implies$ r=$r_{row}$=$r_{{column}}$=2$\implies$ ==$C$==
>2. $R_{0}$ include a part of an identity matrix$\implies$ col1 and col2:independent
>3. col3 of $R_{{0}}$ :reveal how the col3 of $R_{0}$ and $A$ is originated $\implies$ from the perspective of $R_{0}$ : $R$ is $R_{{0}}$ after row3=0 is delated from $R_{0}$ $\implies$ $R$
