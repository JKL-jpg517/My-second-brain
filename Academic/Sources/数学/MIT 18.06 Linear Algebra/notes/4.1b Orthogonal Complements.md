# 4.1b Orthogonal Complements

## Definition

The row space and nullspace are not just orthogonal — they are **orthogonal complements**.

> The orthogonal complement of a subspace $V$, denoted $V^{\perp}$, contains **every** vector that is orthogonal to $V$.

- Nullspace = (Row space)$^{\perp}$
- Row space = (Nullspace)$^{\perp}$
- Left nullspace = (Column space)$^{\perp}$
- Column space = (Left nullspace)$^{\perp}$

## Dimension Formula

The dimensions of orthogonal complements add up to the full space:

$$
\dim(\text{Row space}) + \dim(\text{Nullspace}) = r + (n-r) = n
$$

$$
\dim(\text{Column space}) + \dim(\text{Left nullspace}) = r + (m-r) = m
$$

## Proof of "Every Vector"

**Algebraic argument:**

Nullspace + row space are orthogonal, and their dimensions sum to $n$ → the nullspace already contains *all* vectors orthogonal to the row space. There is no room left for more.

**Geometric argument:**

Both subspaces go through the origin. Once the row space is fixed, the nullspace is determined — it is exactly the set of all directions orthogonal to the row space.

The only vector shared by the two subspaces is the zero vector.
